The Indian cricket team, also known as the "Men in Blue".
The Indian cricket team has a rich history of achievements in both international and domestic cricket. Here are some of the key highlights:

1. ICC Cricket World Cup Victories:

- 1983: India won their first World Cup under the captaincy of Kapil Dev, defeating the West Indies in the final.
- 2011: India, led by MS Dhoni, won their second World Cup by defeating Sri Lanka in the final held in Mumbai.

2. ICC T20 World Cup:

- 2007: India won the inaugural ICC T20 World Cup under the captaincy of MS Dhoni, defeating Pakistan in the final.
-2024: India won the ICC T20 World Cup under the captaincy of Rohit Sharma,defeating South Africa in the final.

3. ICC Champions Trophy:

- 2002: India shared the trophy with Sri Lanka after the final was washed out.
- 2013: India, captained by MS Dhoni, won the ICC Champions Trophy by defeating England in the final.

4. Asia Cup:

- India has won the Asia Cup multiple times, both in the ODI and T20 formats, with victories in 1984, 1988, 1990-91, 1995, 2010, 2016 (T20), and 2018.

5. Test Cricket Achievements:

- India has held the top position in the ICC Test rankings multiple times.
- Notable series victories include winning the Border-Gavaskar Trophy against Australia on several occasions, including a historic series win in Australia in 2018-19 under the captaincy of Virat Kohli and again in 2020-21 under the leadership of Ajinkya Rahane.

6. Individual Records and Achievements:

- Sachin Tendulkar holds numerous records, including the most runs in both ODI and Test cricket and the first player to score a double century in ODIs.
- Anil Kumble is one of the few bowlers to have taken all 10 wickets in a single Test innings.
- Virat Kohli has achieved numerous batting records, including the fastest century in ODIs by an Indian and being the fastest to 8,000, 9,000, 10,000, and 11,000 ODI runs.
